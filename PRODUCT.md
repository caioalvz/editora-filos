# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two overlapping audiences, both arriving with a completed or in-progress manuscript:

- **First-time authors** (fiction, nonfiction, memoir): have a story they believe in but no idea how publishing works. They need reassurance that the process is manageable and that someone credible will guide them.
- **Academic authors** (researchers, professors, PhD candidates): need institutional legitimacy — ISBN, DOI, indexed distribution — and a partner who understands the weight of their work.

Both arrive with a mix of excitement and uncertainty. The site must make publishing feel achievable without underselling the craft involved.

## Product Purpose

Filos Editora turns manuscripts into globally distributed books: editorial design, ISBN and DOI registration, distribution across 40+ platforms, and launch support. The site exists to convert a hesitant author into a confident client — to make the leap from "I wrote something" to "I published something" feel close, professional, and worth investing in.

## Brand Personality

Empowering · modern · accessible

The brand is a skilled enabler: it removes the intimidating complexity of publishing and hands the author a clear path forward. It is not a gatekeeping prestige house. It is not a generic upload-and-print platform. It sits between those poles — serious enough to be trusted, open enough to welcome every kind of author.

## Anti-references

- Amazon KDP, Lulu, Blurb: generic self-pub UI, low craft, commodity feel
- Stuffy legacy publishers: closed, formal, not approachable
- Overproduced "agency" landing pages with hero metrics, gradient text, and glassmorphism

## Design Principles

1. **Excitement before explanation.** The first impression should make the author feel that publishing their book is possible and close — not overwhelm them with process.
2. **Credibility through specificity.** Trust is built by naming real things: 40+ platforms, ISBN, DOI, real testimonials — not by claiming to be "world-class."
3. **Serve both audiences without splitting.** Academic and first-time authors share the same emotional need (validation + clarity); the design should not fragment into two separate voices.
4. **Modern but rooted.** The visual language is contemporary, but books are ancient objects. Typography and texture should carry the weight of that.
5. **Every barrier removed.** CTAs, copy, and flow should make the next step obvious — never vague, never buried.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA strict: all text (including muted/secondary), placeholder text, and icon-only buttons must meet 4.5:1 contrast. Keyboard navigation and focus states required. Reduced-motion alternative for all animations. Primary audience is Brazilian Portuguese speakers.
